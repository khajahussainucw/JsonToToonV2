Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 13/July/2024

Welcome to JsonToTable.org. Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you visit and use our website.

1. Information Collection

JsonToTable.org is designed to convert JSON data into a tabular format, making it easier for you to understand. We do not require registration, and we do not store the JSON data that you input on our site. The data is processed temporarily and is not saved on our servers.

We do not save any JSON data at our servers.

2. Use of Collected Information

The information you provide by entering JSON data is used solely for the purpose of converting it into a table format. Once the conversion process is complete, the data is not saved, stored, or used for any other purposes.

If you choose to contact us through our contact page, we collect the following personal information:

  • Name
  • Email Address

This information is used solely to respond to your inquiries and will not be shared with third parties without your consent. We value your privacy and ensure that any personal information you provide is handled with care.

Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to collect information about how visitors use our site. This may include details about the pages you view, the time you spend on the site, and the actions you take. Google provides further information on their data practices and offers options to control privacy here: Google Privacy & Terms.

Microsoft Clarity: Our use of Microsoft Clarity involves tracking interactions to identify usability issues. All information collected through Clarity is masked and anonymized. No identifiable information is visible to us or stored by Clarity. More details about their privacy practices can be found on their website: Microsoft Clarity.

This Log Data may include information such as your computer's Internet Protocol ("IP") address, browser type, browser version, the pages of our Service that you visit, the time and date of your visit, the time spent on those pages, and other statistics.

5. Cookies

Cookies are files with a small amount of data, which may include an anonymous unique identifier. Cookies are sent to your browser from a website and stored on your computer's hard drive. We use cookies to enhance your experience by remembering your preferences. You can instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent.

While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your Personal Information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security. We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure the data you input on our site is not saved, thereby protecting it from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.
