Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 13/July/2024

Welcome to jsonparser.ai. We take your privacy seriously. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and how we keep it safe when you use our website.

1. Information Collection

Our tool helps you parse and visualize JSON data in different formats. You don't need to sign up or create an account. The JSON you paste into our tool is processed in real-time and never stored on our servers.

We do not save any JSON data at our servers.

2. Use of Collected Information

When you paste JSON data into our tool, we use it only to parse and display it for you. Once you close the page or clear the data, it's gone. We don't save, store, or reuse your JSON for anything else.

If you reach out to us through the contact form, we'll collect:

  • Name
  • Email Address

We only use this info to reply to your message. We won't share it with anyone else unless you give us permission.

3. Analytics and Tracking

We use Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity to analyze website traffic and improve your experience on our site. These tools help us understand user behavior, manage site performance, and identify any issues with site functionality.

Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to collect information about how visitors use our site. This may include details about the pages you view, the time you spend on the site, and the actions you take. Google provides further information on their data practices and offers options to control privacy here: Google Privacy & Terms.

Microsoft Clarity: Our use of Microsoft Clarity involves tracking interactions to identify usability issues. All information collected through Clarity is masked and anonymized. No identifiable information is visible to us or stored by Clarity. More details about their privacy practices can be found on their website: Microsoft Clarity.

4. Log Data

Like many website operators, we collect information that your browser sends whenever you visit our Service ("Log Data"). This Log Data may include information such as your computer's Internet Protocol ("IP") address, browser type, browser version, the pages of our Service that you visit, the time and date of your visit, the time spent on those pages, and other statistics.

5. Cookies

Cookies are tiny files that websites store in your browser to remember things about you. We use them to improve your experience and remember your settings. If you don't want cookies, you can turn them off in your browser settings.

While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your Personal Information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security. We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure the data you input on our site is not saved, thereby protecting it from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.
